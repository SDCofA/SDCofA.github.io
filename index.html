Editorial standard

Name the record before drawing a conclusion.

The label travels with the claim. Source dates and methods stay close enough to the statement for a reader to verify its boundary.

Observation

A dated measurement or source fact.

Observations preserve source, collection time, geography, and the transformation applied. They do not become ground truth merely because a pipeline recorded them.

Assessment

A current interpretation of available evidence.

Assessments explain analytical judgment, known gaps, and the date at which the interpretation was formed. They are not forecasts.

Forecast

A future estimate with a horizon and uncertainty.

Forecasts state their cadence or horizon, method and evidence, assumptions, uncertainty, and limitations. They are estimates—not facts, guarantees, or claims of predictive performance.

Publication discipline

Traceable inputs, declared arithmetic, gated release.

Each product owns its detailed methodology. The common portal standard is intentionally narrow and auditable.

  1. 01

    Collect

    Use lawful public sources and retain source and time context.

  2. 02

    Classify

    Map records to the product's published categories and scope.

  3. 03

    Assess

    Apply the declared method without hiding model or data boundaries.

  4. 04

    Publish or withhold

    Release only after freshness, coverage, and schema gates pass.
